History of Haku

Haku lei "Hah koo lay" or lei po'o "lay poh oh" is a style of flower garland originating in the Polynesian islands where lei giving symbolizes ceremony or rank. Flowers are significant in the culture of Hawai`i, and represent connection to the land and respect towards nature and other people.

Lei making is an art form that blends colorful and textured plants. Given as a greeting or a tribute, lei can be worn at celebrations including birthdays, anniverseries, weddings, graduations, and reunions.
